Abstract

The synthesis of high-quality Multilingual Digital Humans is a transformative advancement for applications in entertainment, education, and virtual communication. To address challenges in achieving robust multilingual performance, high-resolution video synthesis, and expressive animations, we propose an integrated framework combining three state-of-the-art models: CosyVoice 2 for multilingual TTS synthesis, Wan2.1 for diffusion-based video generation, and Wav384 for expressive digital human animation. CosyVoice 2 leverages a supervised speech tokenizer and chunk-aware flow matching to enable scalable, real-time TTS synthesis across diverse languages. Wan2.1 integrates decoupled cross-attention mechanisms with CLIP and umT5 encoders to generate temporally consistent and semantically aligned videos. Wav384 employs a pre-trained lip-sync expert and skip-connection architectures to ensure natural expressions and precise speech-to-lip synchronization. Together, these models deliver high-definition Multilingual Digital Humans that adapt seamlessly to multilingual and multimodal scenarios, enabling applications such as live-streaming, personalized content creation, and immersive virtual communication. This work sets a new benchmark for Multilingual Digital Human synthesis, bridging linguistic and cultural barriers in the Metaverse.
Keywords- Multilingual Digital Humans, Multilingual Text-to-Speech (TTS), Diffusion-Based Video Generation, Speech-to-Lip Synchronization, Expressive Animations, Virtual Communication, Personalized Content Creation, Live-Streaming, Metaverse, Cross-Linguistic Applications
